About Me

Hi! I'm Sid. I'm a student at UCSD, and I study bioinformatics, math, and computer science. A couple of my hobbies are surfing, math, reading, and baking. I'm really curious about how the world works, and so here you'll (eventually) find my projects, which are directed lines of inquiry about topics that I find interesting, and explorables, which are interactive, accessible explanations of those same topics.

Influences and Inspirations

I've picked up a lot of my views about understanding the world and communicating from a couple of influential people. Here are just a few:
  • Steven Wittens' fantastic blog was the reason I developed a passion for math.
  • Bret Victor has done a lot of writing about better media for explanation and communication.
  • Nicky Case has a blog that puts a lot of these ideas into practice.
  • Grant Sanderson's Youtube channel, 3Blue1Brown, is also a great channel for making light of math.

Goals

I'm drawing from Explorables here - first and foremost, I want this to be about explanation through play. I've got a bunch of my own projects which I hope to turn into full-fledged explorables soon enough.

Aside from being a creative outlet for me, some subordinate goals I have for this website are accessibility and design. First off, you'll notice that I'm writing this website in semantic HTML (to the degree that I can) in order to try to make this site accessible to those who use a screen reader, or a keyboard for navigation instead of a mouse. Often times in my previous work design has come at a cost to accessibility, which I intend to avoid on this site.

Hi! I'm Sid. I'm a student at UCSD, and I study bioinformatics, math, and computer science. A couple of my hobbies are surfing, math, reading, and climbing. I'm really curious about how the world works, and I hope to use this blog to document and compile what I read and see.

Influences and Inspirations

I've picked up a lot of my views about understanding the world and communicating from a couple of influential people. Here are just a few:
  • Steven Wittens' fantastic posts about fractals and calculus were among the reasons I developed a passion for math.
  • Bret Victor has done a lot of writing about better media for explanation and communication.
  • Nicky Case has a blog that puts a lot of these ideas into practice.
  • Grant Sanderson's Youtube channel, 3Blue1Brown, is also a great channel for making light of math.

Goals

I'm drawing from Explorables here - first and foremost, I want this website to be about explanation through play. I've got a bunch of my own projects which I hope to turn into full-fledged explorables.

Aside from being a creative outlet for me, some subordinate goals I have for this website are accessibility and design. First off, you'll notice that I'm writing this website in semantic HTML (to the degree that I can) in order to try to make this site accessible to those who use a screen reader, or a keyboard for navigation instead of a mouse. Often times in my previous work design has come at a cost to accessibility, which I hope to avoid on this site.
